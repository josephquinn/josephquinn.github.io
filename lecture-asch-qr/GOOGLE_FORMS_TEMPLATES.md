# Google Forms Templates

These are fallback templates, not the primary recommendation. Google Forms does not natively do 80/20 random assignment, so the static page should handle assignment and message delivery.

If you still want two forms ready to go, create them with the text below.

## Form 1: Confederate instructions

**Suggested title**

`SOC 101 Private Instructions`

**Suggested description**

`This page contains private instructions for the next classroom task. Keep your phone angled away from other students. Do not show this screen or discuss it out loud.`

**Questions**

1. `Email address`
   - Type: `Short answer`
   - Required: `Yes`

2. `Private instructions`
   - Type: `Multiple choice`
   - Required: `Yes`
   - Prompt:

`KEEP THIS MESSAGE FROM YOUR PEERS AROUND YOU. Do not show your screen, and do not discuss this message with anyone nearby. You are a secret agent in the next task. Do not tell anyone around you. On the next slide, say that B is the correct answer. Do not be overconfident. Do not smirk or laugh. Do not fold. Agree with anyone who agrees with you.`

   - Choices:
     - `I have read and understood these private instructions.`

**Suggested confirmation message**

`Your private message has been cleared. Look up at the next lecture slide.`

## Form 2: Control warm-up

**Suggested title**

`SOC 101 Private Warm-Up`

**Suggested description**

`This page contains a private warm-up question for the next classroom task. Keep your phone angled away from other students. Do not show this screen or discuss it out loud.`

**Questions**

1. `Email address`
   - Type: `Short answer`
   - Required: `Yes`

2. `Private warm-up question`
   - Type: `Multiple choice`
   - Required: `Yes`
   - Prompt:

`KEEP THIS MESSAGE FROM YOUR PEERS AROUND YOU. Do not show your screen, and do not discuss this message with anyone nearby. This is a private warm-up question. Which sociologist is most closely associated with strain theory?`

   - Choices:
     - `A. William Graham Sumner`
     - `B. Robert K. Merton`
     - `C. Howard S. Becker`
     - `D. Erich Goode`

**Suggested confirmation message**

`Your private message has been cleared. Look up at the next lecture slide.`

## How to use these forms

- If you want a no-code backup, make both forms in advance and keep the links handy.
- If you want random assignment, the custom page in this folder should still be the first step.
- That page can assign the condition, display the message itself, or be modified later to redirect students into one of these two forms.
